My fitness journey

My fitness journey began in September 2021 when I arrived at UCR.

1st year: One goal I had set for myself when attending UCR was to get in better shape. I began doing cardio in order to lose some unwanted fat. I soon switched to weight machines to perform both upper and lower body exercises. I made it a priority to go every day for about an hour. I stuck to this goal and made tremendous progress. I went from about 180-190 lbs.

2nd year: I was not as consistent with going to the gym everyday, but I would still go about three times a week. I continued the same routine that I was doing the previous year. During this time I went from 190-210 lbs.

3rd year: This was the year where I made the most progress both in and out of the gym. I was determined to finish college in three years, which meant that I needed to take a heavier course load with five classes for two quarters in order to complete senior design. In addition to this workload on the academic side, I wanted to shed some fat, which was one of my original goals from year one. During this time, I primarily focused on upper body exercises. I also started taking into consideration the nutritional value that foods have. I continued going to the gym about three times a week. I also added more free weight exercises to my routine. Eventually, I was able to perform pull ups without assistance, a lifelong dream. When I started doing pull ups in year one, I needed the maximum amount of assistance on the machine. As far as weight goes, I went from 210-150 lbs. This was more than I expected that I would be able to accomplish. I was finally able to fit into a lot of my old clothes. The change in my appearance was so significant that people started to take notice and congratulated me on my progress.
